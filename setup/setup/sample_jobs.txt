About Springdale Industries

Springdale Industries ("Springdale") was founded by Frank Zhang and Jake Sloane in 2025 to partner with small and medium size business owner-operators to scale their local businesses with the support of a national platform. Springdale supports local businesses that thrive independently while gaining resources, systems, and leadership to accelerate growth and profitability.

Prior to founding Springdale, Jake & Frank founded six companies together, including:
Crete Professionals Alliance: Accounting and professional services platform; strategic capital from Thrive Capital and Bessemer Venture Partners
Rocket Youth Brands: Youth enrichment platform; strategic capital from Maverick Carter and Daniel Sillman
Shield Technology Partners: IT managed services provider; strategic capital from Thrive Capital
Crete Mechanical Group: Commercial mechanical services platform majority-owned by Ridgemont Equity Partners today
Legacy Service Partners: Residential services platform majority-owned by Gridiron Capital today
Alliance Animal Health: Veterinary services platform majority-owned by L Catterton today

Over the past 10 years, these companies have completed 250+ transactions and integrations and created over $4 billion in consolidated total enterprise value.

Position Summary
We often say, "We are in the integration business." Integrations are the core of our growth model: "BIG" (Buy, Integrate, Grow). Springdale's Partner Enablement function builds the systems, playbooks, and cultural muscle that turn newly acquired partners into thriving members of the Springdale platform.

The Director of Partner Enablement is Springdale's embedded strategic operator within its newer, less mature platforms, effectively serving as the acting head of Partner Enablement at each platform until it reaches the scale and stability to justify a dedicated, full-time hire. This is a general-manager-style mandate: the Director diagnoses what each platform needs to support its partners, designs the onboarding and enablement approach that fits its specific situation, and owns building the function from the ground up.

The Director of Partner Enablement will partner closely with the VP of Partner Enablement and platform leadership to shape partner strategy and translate it into durable systems. This role is best suited to a builder who wants outsized ownership early, someone who thrives at the intersection of strategy and hands-on execution and who wants a running start on a general management or operating career inside a fast-growing platform business.

Key Responsibilities
Diagnose & Design Platform Strategy: Assess each platform's opportunities and risk areas at launch and design an approach best suited to that platform.
Serve as Embedded Partner Enablement Lead: Act as the de facto head of Partner Enablement within newer platforms, owning the onboarding relationship with newly acquired partners end-to-end.
Architect Platform Operating Infrastructure: Build the operating backbone that gives each platform a durable, transparent home for onboarding activity.
Lead Enablement and Change Management Strategy: Design and deliver a roadmap for each platform that supports partner growth and drives adoption of new processes, systems, and tools.
Drive Cross-Functional & Executive Alignment: Act as the connective tissue between platform leadership, corporate functions, and Springdale's senior team.
Institutionalize the Playbook Feedback Loop: Apply Springdale's playbook in the field and incorporate real-world learnings into the broader playbook.
Automate & Scale the Function: Partner with the VP and Tech team to build automation that lets the function scale disproportionate to headcount.

Position Requirements
3-6+ years at a top-tier management consulting firm (McKinsey, Bain, BCG, or similar), or comparable experience in private equity, investment banking, or a high-growth operating environment.
Demonstrated ability to diagnose ambiguous problems and design a strategic approach.
Comfort building a function from scratch, owning outcomes end-to-end, and moving fluidly between strategic and hands-on work.
Strong executive presence, able to earn credibility with platform CEOs and operators from day one.
Comfort with ambiguity and a fast-moving, evolving function.
Prior operations or business-operator experience a plus, but not required.
